Where and When Crimes Happen in Los Angeles (2023–2024)

Crime is a major concern in large cities like Los Angeles. This page explores crime patterns using data from 2023 to 2024.

The full data analysis process is available in the Jupyter Notebook .

All visualizations are created using data from the Los Angeles Open Data Portal.

Where do most crimes occur?

Some areas report significantly higher crime counts than others. However, this may also reflect population differences. Crime is not evenly distributed across Los Angeles. Areas such as Central, Pacific, and Southwest report significantly higher numbers of incidents. This pattern likely reflects population density, commercial activity, and the concentration of public spaces where more interactions occur.

Data source: Los Angeles Open Data Portal (Crime Data)

What types of crimes are most common?

This chart shows the ten most common reported crime types in Los Angeles from 2023 to 2024. It helps readers see which categories make up the largest share of reported incidents. Property-related crimes dominate the dataset, with vehicle theft and burglary appearing among the most common categories. This suggests that everyday economic and opportunity-driven crimes make up a large share of reported incidents.

Who are the reported victims?

This chart shows the distribution of reported victims by recorded sex category. The dataset uses F, M, and X categories, where X generally represents unknown or non-specified records. Because these labels depend on how incidents were reported and recorded, they should be interpreted carefully. The distribution of victims shows that both male and female individuals are frequently represented in crime reports, while a portion of records are categorized as unknown. These categories depend on how incidents are recorded and may not fully reflect the true demographic distribution.

How did crime reports change over time?

This line chart shows monthly reported crime counts from 2023 to 2024. Overall, crime levels appear relatively stable throughout most of 2023, fluctuating around a similar range. However, there is a noticeable decline starting in early 2024, with reported incidents dropping significantly over time. This downward trend may reflect changes in reporting practices, seasonal variation, or incomplete data for later months in 2024. It is important to interpret this pattern cautiously, as the apparent decrease could be influenced by data collection or reporting delays rather than a true reduction in crime.
